In this part, I am glad to share my personal stuff with you.

I love languages, and I have discovered how to write my name in different languages. You can find my name in other languages here.

Although I am interested in languages, I am not a polyglot. However, I can sing songs of 13 languages: English, German, Spanish, Russian, Ukrainian, Hebrew, Arabic, Hindi, Chinese, Japanese, Tagalog, Indonesian, and Vietnamese.

A language always connects with its culture. Indeed I am actually interested in meeting people from various countries and living in a multicultural environment. That's the reason I choose Canada as my second country. I have been in Canada since February 2021, and I really like hospitable people in Sudbury, either her residents or immigrants. Of course, I am very fond of the nature here as well.

I myself find that being able to use various languages is advantageous, though at only basic level. My first part-time job in Canada came from a suggestion of a Brazilian lady who only speaks Brazilian Portuguese and does not know English. She introduced me to a Peruvian cleaning supervisor who can speak Spanish, Portuguese, and English. This is an amazing experience, particularly when I was unprepared to communicate in Portuguese, because my small research before moving here showed that less than 1% people in Sudbury speak Portuguese.


This is my photo taken in the lunch room at Best Buy Sudbury. You can notice the cultural diversity in just a company. Now imagine the whole Sudbury, or even Canada.


Walking on the street in Sudbury, I can easily catch Canadians, Indians, and Nigerians, but rarely East Asians or Vietnamese people. In fact, Vietnamese people are among the smallest ethnic minorities here. For several months since I have lived in Sudbury, my Vietnamese friends have been merely students who lived in the same apartment. One day in October 2021, a person came across my Facebook post and he invited me to a meeting about preparing for Tet in 2022 (also known as Lunar New Year). He is the vice president of Vietnamese Association Sudbury (VAS). In that meeting, I was elected secretary of VAS with a duty of managing its social media and connecting the Vietnamese youth with senior Vietnamese.
